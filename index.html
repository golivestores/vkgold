Our Craft

From sacred tradition to modern precision — every piece follows a meticulous journey.

Step 01
Sacred Preparation
Before a single stroke is made, the master performs purification rituals — bathing, chanting sutras, and seeking blessings. Every painting begins with reverence.
Step 02
Canvas & Sketch
Handmade cotton canvas, treated with chalk gesso. The composition follows strict Buddhist scripture proportions — every posture and gesture precisely prescribed.
Step 03
Mineral Pigments
Malachite, azurite, cinnabar, gold and silver — hand-ground into fine pigments. Layers applied gradually over weeks, building depth that endures for centuries.
Step 04
Gold Leaf & Fine Lines
24K gold leaf laid stroke by stroke — the "pujin" technique. Ultra-fine contour lines and silver accents transform the painting into a luminous, living icon.
Step 05
Eye-Opening & Consecration
The most sacred moment: painting the deity's eyes — "kai yan". The scroll is mounted in silk brocade and consecrated by a Buddhist master.
Silver Foil Craft
Step 06
PVD Silver Deposition
99.9% pure silver optically sputtered onto PET substrate via Physical Vapour Deposition — achieving the most uniform thickness for every foil artwork.
Step 07
Precision Pressing
Taiwanese cold press + Japanese hot press, temperature controlled to ±1°C. Senior technicians monitor in real time for flawless embossing.
Step 08
Artisan Finishing
Skilled artisans hand-adjust every edge — smoothing surfaces, sharpening relief details, softening contours to a flowing elegance no machine can replicate.
Step 09
UV Protection & Printing
UV coating seals silver from oxidation. Computer-analysed colour data guides precision printing — monitored live for vivid, gap-free fidelity.
