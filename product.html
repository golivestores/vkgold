Symbolism & Meaning

Every Blessing Carries a Story

Each design in the VKGold blessing series draws from a specific deity, mountain, or sacred lineage — chosen not for decoration, but for the centuries of devotion and spiritual power they embody. Carried daily on your phone, these are not ornaments. They are intentions made tangible in 99.9% pure gold.

Wealth & Prosperity

Wutai Dragon King

The Dragon King of Wutai Mountain (五台山龙王) is the supreme guardian of material abundance in Chinese Buddhism. Wutai — one of the Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains — is believed to be the earthly abode of Manjushri Bodhisattva, where the Dragon King controls rain, harvest, and the flow of fortune. Carrying his image invites steady wealth, business growth, and the dissolution of financial obstacles. Traditionally sought by entrepreneurs, merchants, and anyone entering a new venture.

Wisdom & Career

Wudang Wenchang Dijun

Wenchang Dijun (文昌帝君) is the celestial ruler of literature, scholarship, and examinations — worshipped for over a thousand years across China. His origin traces to the Wudang Mountains, seat of Taoist cultivation and martial arts mastery. Devotees believe he governs the fate of all who sit for exams, apply for positions, or seek promotion. His blessing sharpens the mind, clears mental fog, and opens doors to academic honours and professional recognition. Favoured by students, scholars, and career-driven professionals.

Love & Protection

Zaki Ram (Tashi Lhamo)

Zaki Ram (扎基拉姆), also known as Tashi Lhamo, is the only female wealth deity in Tibetan Buddhism — and one of the most fiercely beloved protectors in the Himalayan world. Originally a wrathful emanation of Palden Lhamo, she guards the Zaki Temple in Lhasa, where pilgrims offer white wine and butter to seek her favour. Unlike other wealth gods, Zaki Ram’s blessings extend beyond money: she protects romantic bonds, family harmony, feminine beauty, and shields her devotees from jealousy and malice. She is especially called upon by women and those navigating matters of the heart.
