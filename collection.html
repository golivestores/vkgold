Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our sacred art, gold blessings, and the craft behind every piece.

Ask Us on WhatsApp →
Thangka (also spelled Tangka or Thanka) is a traditional Tibetan Buddhist scroll painting, created on cotton or silk using natural mineral pigments and 24K gold leaf. Each piece follows strict iconographic rules passed down through centuries of lineage. Our Thangka paintings are hand-painted by UNESCO Intangible Heritage masters Quzhi and Zhaxijiancuo from Regong, Qinghai — one of the few remaining centres of this ancient art.
Each blessing sticker begins with 99.9% pure silver, deposited onto a high-transparency PET substrate using Physical Vapour Deposition (PVD) sputtering technology. The silver foil is then shaped through a hot-press and cold-press process using precision machinery from Taiwan and Japan. Finally, 99.9% pure gold is applied, followed by UV protection coating and special colour printing — all verified by hand at every stage.
A standard Nine Bodhisattva series Thangka requires a minimum of 15 working days. Larger or more complex commissions — such as multi-deity compositions or full 24K gold backgrounds — may take 30 to 90 days. Every painting undergoes sacred preparation rituals, pigment grinding, gold leaf application, the eye-opening ceremony, and silk brocade mounting before delivery.
